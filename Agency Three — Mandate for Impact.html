SYSTEM
Root causes of failure

Why do many CIO appointments fail before they have even started?

The risk usually appears before the offer is made: in the brief, in the assessment and in the system into which the leader will land.

01

The brief is weak

The organisation has ambition but has not translated it into the leadership conditions required to deliver.

02

The assessment is incomplete

Candidates are judged on reputation, presentation and past roles rather than evidence against the real mandate.

03

The landing pad is unprepared

The organisation is not clear on the authority, sponsorship and decision rights required to deliver the mandate. Without that clarity, even a strong leader inherits structural failure.

Pitch and catch

A successful CIO appointment is not just about the quality of the pitch.

It is also about the quality of the catch.

The leader brings

  • Capability
  • Judgement
  • Experience
  • Energy
  • Execution discipline

The organisation must provide

  • Sponsorship
  • Decision rights
  • Constructive challenge
  • Engaged peers
  • Realistic measures of success
Hidden failure point

Bad engagement creates bad outcomes.

Many CIO failures are created by the system around the role: unclear sponsorship, wrong incentives and decision rights that do not match the mandate.

01

Wrong incentives

Leaders are asked to transform, but rewarded for avoiding disruption.

02

Weak sponsorship

The organisation wants change, but no one protects the difficult decisions required to deliver it.

03

Poor catch

The leader may be capable, but the executive system is not ready to engage with them properly.

It’s an almost impossible task without the right support

The role

The high consequence Global CIO mandate.

In a large scale global enterprise, the CIO role is no longer about running technology. It is about translating business ambition into an executable technology agenda, leading enterprise-wide change, protecting resilience and giving the board confidence that technology is creating value rather than complexity.

01
Translate commercial ambition into a funded technology roadmap

Not a project list. A board level story about where technology takes the business, what it will cost and what the CIO can be held accountable for delivering.

02
Own the most complex change programme in the organisation

A transformation agenda that touches every function, requires sustained CEO commitment and becomes the most visible test of the Global CIO.

03
Hold ultimate accountability for cyber and resilience

Ensure the organisation can withstand attacks and satisfy an increasingly demanding global regulatory environment.

04
Separate genuine AI advantage from noise and cost

Move fast enough to capture opportunity, but with the judgement to avoid expensive moves in the wrong direction.

05
Reduce decades of technical debt without breaking the business

Create a credible path through systems nobody fully understands, that everything depends on and that make change dangerously slow.

06
Build a technology function that can deliver at business pace

Attract and retain scarce talent, making corporate technology a destination rather than a consolation prize.

07
Make enterprise data usable at scale

Solve governance, quality and literacy challenges that are as much cultural as they are technical.

08
Give the board a language it can challenge and govern

Build a relationship with the Chair and non executives that goes well beyond the annual technology update.

The risk is systemic

How the process works

A structured route from enterprise context to appointment impact.

The process is deliberately simple: align the organisation around the real mandate, diagnose what success requires, assess candidates against that reality and prepare the conditions for impact.

01

Independent conversations with the CEO, CHRO, CFO and board/NED surface ambition, tension and reality.

02

Diagnose

Translate those inputs into a mandate: maturity, risk, AI/data/cyber posture, decision rights and success conditions.

03

Assess

04

Land

Prepare the CEO, board and executive team to catch the leader properly and review whether appointment is converting into impact.

Brief diagnostic

The questions most briefs rarely answer.

  • What is the board genuinely asking this leader to deliver?
  • What is the honest AI, data and cyber posture?
  • How much executive sponsorship really exists?
  • What change can the organisation actually absorb?
  • Where does this leader have genuine decision rights?
  • Where will the organisation resist the mandate?
  • What does success look like in 6, 12 and 18 months?
  • What must the CEO and board do differently?
