Most organisations know the ambition. Fewer have translated it into the leadership conditions required to deliver it.

“The brief that feels current to those constructing it may already be behind the curve in terms of what the market actually demands.”

Six questions the brief rarely answers
01
What is the board genuinely asking this leader to deliver?
Not the aspirational version; the specific, measurable mandate for the first 12 to 18 months.
02
What is the organisation's current technology maturity?
Is it a foundation the incoming leader can build on, or a constraint they will first need to resolve?
03

06
What does the AI, data and cyber posture require on day one?
Not in twelve months; not eventually. What is the minimum viable leadership position this person needs to hold from the moment they arrive?

The right leader for one organisation often fails in another when the context changes.

Every organisation is different. Technology maturity, board sponsorship, culture, change readiness, governance architecture; the right appointment is deeply contextual. Generic assessment models were not built for this. Neither were unstructured interviews. And instinct has never been a reliable substitute for evidence.

How we are solving this; Step 1 of 5

Before any candidate is considered, we help you build the right leadership brief: one that captures the ambition, the complexity and the capabilities required to succeed.

Agency Three conducts structured briefing conversations with each key stakeholder independently. CEO, CFO, CHRO and Board member perspectives are captured through a role-specific questionnaire designed to surface what each stakeholder genuinely needs; not what they assume the others want to hear. The results are then synthesised into a single, agreed mandate brief.

Built from 7,000+ senior technology leader interviews. EQ + IQ = XQ.

A leader can be strategically brilliant and still fail to create change. They can be emotionally intelligent and still lack the judgement to operate at enterprise level.

The best technology leaders are not defined by either dimension alone, but by the way they combine both to drive execution. That combination; what Agency Three calls XQ, is what most hiring processes completely fail to measure.
